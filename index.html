ICDepth: Taming Video Diffusion Models for Video Depth Estimation via In-Context Conditioning

Accepted by ECCV 2026
Xuanhua He* Jiaxin Xie* Mingzhe Zheng Qifeng Chen†
The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology
* Equal contribution.   † Corresponding author.

Abstract

Monocular video depth estimation requires temporal consistency, geometric accuracy, and generalization. We introduce ICDepth, a framework adapting pre-trained Text-to-Video Diffusion Transformers for depth estimation via In-Context Conditioning (ICC). To address key challenges in transferring ICC to dense prediction, we propose:

  • SAND-Attention (Spatial-temporal Aligned, Noise-Decoupled Attention), which ensures precise alignment via shared RoPE and prevents noise contamination.
  • SRFM (Semantic- and Resolution-Aware Feature Modulation), which injects DINOv2 and resolution priors to enhance geometric precision.

ICDepth achieves state-of-the-art results on multiple benchmarks while being trained on only 0.8M frames—6-13x less data than competing methods.

Method

Our framework builds upon the Wanx 2.1 VDiT. We treat RGB and depth as a unified token sequence processed by native attention.

1. SAND-Attention: Establish precise spatial-temporal alignment between clean RGB conditions and noisy depth latents through Shared RoPE. Furthermore, it enforces Unidirectional Attention, allowing noisy depth tokens to query clean RGB tokens while blocking the reverse flow.

2. SRFM: Inject powerful external priors into the generative model. We leverage DINOv2 features for rich semantic understanding and Resolution Embeddings to support multi-scale inference (handling varying aspect ratios up to 1080p).
